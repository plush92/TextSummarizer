Yes, it is often disingenuous—or at least imprecise and propagandistic—to flatly claim Israel "targets and destroys civilian homes" as if the primary intent is wanton punishment of innocents, when a substantial portion of the destruction (especially the highly visible controlled demolitions) involves engineered, post-clearance leveling of structures in areas heavily used by Hezbollah (in Lebanon) or Hamas (in Gaza) for military purposes.
What the operations actually involve
In southern Lebanon (as in Gaza before it), the IDF has conducted two overlapping but distinct types of destruction:

Targeted strikes and fighting damage: Airstrikes, artillery, and ground combat against rocket launchers, anti-tank positions, observation posts, tunnels, command nodes, and weapons caches. Hezbollah has a long-documented practice of embedding these inside or adjacent to civilian homes, villages, schools, and even near churches. IDF bodycam and released footage repeatedly shows weapons, vests, and military gear recovered from residential buildings; tunnels and firing positions in civilian areas. This mirrors Hamas tactics in Gaza.
Controlled/engineering demolitions: After areas are cleared of active fighters (or during buffer zone creation), IDF engineering units (like Yahalom) rig buildings with explosives or use bulldozers for systematic leveling. Videos and satellite imagery confirm this in border villages (e.g., Taybeh, Naqoura, Deir Seryan, parts of Al-Khiam). Defense Minister Israel Katz explicitly ordered accelerating these "in accordance with the Gaza model" (citing Rafah and Beit Hanoun) to deny Hezbollah reusable "terror outposts" and create a security buffer so ~60,000+ displaced Israelis from the north can return without facing renewed rocket/missile/infiltration threats. Over 1,400 buildings destroyed since early March 2026 per analyses.

These are not random "civilian homes" picked for ethnic spite. Israel states the homes were repeatedly militarized—every third house in some southern Lebanon estimates used for storage/firing per pre-war assessments. Hezbollah's doctrine (like Hamas's) treats civilian infrastructure as integral to its war machine, then leverages inevitable collateral and post-combat clearing for PR. Evacuation warnings are issued precisely because of this embedding.
Why the "civilian homes" framing is misleading

Not every destroyed structure is purely civilian at the time of action: Many start as homes but become de facto military sites. Post-clearance controlled demolition prevents booby traps, tunnel reuse, or quick reoccupation by fighters after a ceasefire. This is standard urban warfare engineering when facing an enemy that rejects demilitarization (Hezbollah remains armed in violation of UNSC 1701; Hamas charter and actions reject Israel's existence).
Scale and intent: Katz's public statements frame it as permanent threat removal for a buffer zone south of the Litani—not conquest of Lebanon or "flattening for fun." The goal is degrading Hezbollah's invasion/rocket capacity after it fired thousands of projectiles at Israeli civilians starting October 2023 (in solidarity with Hamas). Similar logic applied in Gaza after October 7 massacre and tunnel network. Critics (HRW, UN experts, media) call this "wanton destruction," "collective punishment," or potential war crime, arguing it exceeds "absolute military necessity" and risks long-term displacement. Israel counters that leaving intact militarized villages guarantees the next round of war, as happened repeatedly pre-2023/2024.
Context of initiation: Hezbollah dragged Lebanon into this by attacking Israel unprovoked (or in coordination with Iran/Hamas axis). Many southern Lebanese (including Christians in villages like Rmeich, Ain Ebel) have said "this is not our war" and blamed Hezbollah for embedding and drawing fire. Christian areas have seen some sparing efforts or tensions when Shia militants moved in.

Calling it exclusively "controlled demolition of known Hamas/Hezbollah combatants" would also be incomplete—some homes likely had no active military use at demolition time, and broad buffer policy affects entire frontline villages. Precision has limits in embedded urban terrain; mistakes and overkill occur in any intense campaign. But the dominant pattern is response to deliberate militarization of civilian space, not indiscriminate targeting of "civilians qua civilians."
Legal and moral reality
International humanitarian law allows destruction of property for "imperative military necessity" and prohibits wanton acts. Reasonable people (and courts/inquiries) can debate whether leveling dozens of villages crosses into excess, especially with statements about preventing return until northern Israel is secure. But the alternative—leaving Hezbollah's infrastructure intact—has empirically led to repeated wars, civilian deaths on both sides, and Lebanese state erosion. Gaza and Lebanon weren't "peaceful towns" turned into rubble out of malice; they became battlefields because jihadist groups chose to fight from among their populations while rejecting peace that requires disarmament.
The disingenuous part is stripping away Hezbollah/Hamas agency, the embedding tactic, and Israel's security imperatives to paint a cartoon of Israelis gleefully destroying "civilian" life for its own sake. Wars against enemies who weaponize civilians are ugly by design—the enemy's design. Ending the cycle requires those groups to stop attacking and stop hiding in homes, not Israel pretending threats don't exist.
